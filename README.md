# High Voltage I - Laboratory Reports (AUTh ECE)

This repository contains the laboratory reports and data analysis for the **High Voltage I** course, conducted during the 7th semester of the Integrated Master's program at the Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering, Aristotle University of Thessaloniki (AUTh). 

The reports combine advanced power engineering theory, laboratory experimental procedures, and analytical modeling. All documentation is authored in **LaTeX** and data processing/visualization is performed via **MATLAB**.

## 📋 Repository Structure

The repository includes comprehensive reports for the following laboratory modules:

*   **Reports 1 & 2: Introduction to HV Testing & The Ferranti Effect**[cite: 4]
    *   Analysis of safety regulations in high-voltage environments and Faraday cage implementations[cite: 4].
    *   Experimental verification and numerical calculation of the Ferranti effect under capacitive loading using single and cascaded testing transformers[cite: 4].
*   **Report 3: Generation and Measurement of HVAC**[cite: 5]
    *   Comparative study of HVAC measurement methodologies: Davies-Bowdler-Standring, Chubb-Fortescue, Voltage Dividers (Ohmic/Capacitive), and Sphere Gaps[cite: 5].
    *   Evaluation of stray capacitances and geometric breakdown conditions[cite: 5].
*   **Report 4: Generation and Measurement of HVDC**[cite: 1]
    *   Analysis of rectifying topologies: Half-wave rectification, Villard, Greinacher, and Cockcroft-Walton multi-stage multipliers[cite: 1].
    *   Ripple factor calculations and efficiency analysis under distinct resistive loads[cite: 1].
*   **Report 5: Generation and Measurement of Impulse High Voltages (LI / SI)**[cite: 2]
    *   Single-stage Marx generator modeling (Type-a and Type-b circuits)[cite: 2].
    *   Testing of Standard Lightning Impulses ($1.2/50\ \mu s$) and Switching Impulses ($250/2500\ \mu s$)[cite: 2].
    *   Controlled triggering optimization using a Trigatron spark gap device[cite: 2].
*   **Report 6: Generation and Measurement of High Impulse Currents**[cite: 3]
    *   Mathematical modeling of RLC discharge paths (underdamped, critically damped, and overdamped responses)[cite: 3].
    *   Testing of standard surge currents ($8/20\ \mu s$ and $10/350\ \mu s$) on surge protection devices (Strikesorb SPD)[cite: 3].

## 🛠️ Tools & Methodologies Used

*   **Document Preparation:** LaTeX (with custom PGF/TikZ or pgfplots vector graphics for precise engineering reports).
*   **Data Analysis & Modeling:** MATLAB scripts for differential equation parsing (Frobenius method tracking, transcendental timing extractions), and curve fitting between experimental data and theoretical waveshapes
